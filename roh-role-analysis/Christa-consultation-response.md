## Position statement

### Consultation Response – Concerns Regarding Proposed E&F Support Officer Role

I would like to formally outline my concerns regarding the proposed E&F Support Officer role and why I do not currently believe I have sufficient information to determine whether this role constitutes suitable alternative employment.

I would also like to confirm that I am continuing to engage fully in the consultation process and that my application for voluntary redundancy was submitted in order to protect my position while significant questions remain unanswered. This should not be interpreted as a refusal to engage with consultation or to reasonably consider alternative employment.

### Whether the Role Is Sufficiently Similar

The restructure is wide-ranging. The role should not be treated as an automatic direct match without fuller assessment.

I recognise that some elements of my current role naturally overlap with the proposed role, particularly administrative and coordination-based functions. My concern is not that there is no overlap, but that the proposed structure materially changes reporting lines, who signs off work, and practical expectations attached to those functions.

Although there are some overlapping tasks, how the role would work day to day appears materially different.

My current role as Facilities Coordinator includes:

- contractor coordination and commercial oversight,
- operational coordination responsibilities,
- budget monitoring and reporting,
- facilities management support,
- project and compliance support,
- coordination across departments with sign-off Christa holds now.

The proposed E&F Support Officer role appears significantly more focused on:

- helpdesk support,
- reactive service requests,
- inbox management,
- administrative processing,
- CAFM updates,
- customer service and support functions.

The overall emphasis appears to have shifted from independent operational coordination toward helpdesk administration, reactive support, and tasks carried out within an integrated team with escalation to a Manager.

I therefore do not believe the role reflects the same level of sign-off or day-to-day accountability as my current position.

### Operational scope and accountability

I remain concerned that the proposed JD does not preserve the coordinator remit I currently hold.

In my view:

- accountability and sign-off arrangements would change materially under the Support Manager line,
- helpdesk and administrative support functions would increase substantially, and
- several coordinator duties in my current JD are absent or reframed as assist/support tasks in the proposed JD.

### Missing Operational Detail Before VR Deadline

A significant concern is that I was asked to assess suitability and make decisions regarding my employment position before key operational terms of the proposed role had been defined.

During consultation I was advised that:

- workload distribution,
- team structure,
- office attendance expectations,
- helpdesk arrangements,
- and operational processes

cannot be clarified until the new manager is appointed in mid-to-late June, and that how work would be shared would be discussed once the manager was in post.

However, the deadline for voluntary redundancy applications was before this information would be available.

I do not believe this has allowed employees to properly assess whether the role would be suitable.

Without clarity regarding how the role will actually operate day-to-day, including helpdesk coverage between 8am and 6pm, office attendance, and workload split, I do not feel fully informed enough to assess suitability.

### Flexible Working and Office Attendance

At the beginning of consultation, employees were advised that existing flexible working arrangements would not change.

However, I was later informed that future office attendance expectations would be determined by the new manager.

This causes concern because my current hybrid working arrangement is important in supporting my childcare responsibilities.

At present:

- I work part-time,
- I work both from home and in the office,
- and my current arrangement is structured around childcare commitments.

I therefore do not feel reassured that the proposed role would continue to support my existing flexible working arrangements.

### Helpdesk Hours and Part-Time Working

The proposed role includes supporting a helpdesk operating between 8am and 6pm.

However, there is currently no clarity regarding:

- how this would operate for part-time employees,
- how sickness cover would work,
- whether employees would be expected to work outside contracted days,
- or how staffing gaps would be managed.

As a part-time employee with childcare responsibilities, this uncertainty is particularly concerning.

I do not believe sufficient information has been provided regarding how these operational requirements would work in practice.

### Workload and Merged Role

It has been stated that the purpose of the restructure is to streamline processes and reduce duplication between teams.

However, my concern is that combining support, helpdesk, and operational responsibilities into one role may significantly increase workload and reduce the effectiveness of the function overall.

This further contributes to my concerns regarding the practicality and sustainability of the role structure.

### Response to overlap in duties

I recognise that I already perform overlapping functions in my current role, including access control coordination, contractor liaison, purchase orders, SharePoint maintenance, and general coordination tasks.

My concern is not the wording alone but how those functions would operate in practice: within an integrated team with escalation to a Manager, rather than with the sign-off and accountability I currently hold. Similar tasks in the proposed job description do not amount to the same role if who signs off work and day-to-day expectations are materially different.

### Voluntary Redundancy Application

I submitted an application for voluntary redundancy because:

- I do not currently have enough information to properly assess whether the role is suitable,
- key operational details remain unknown,
- and there remain unresolved concerns regarding how the role would work in practice, flexibility, and compatibility with my working arrangements.

My application for voluntary redundancy should not be interpreted as:

- refusing to engage in consultation,
- refusing to consider alternative employment,
- or unreasonably rejecting a suitable role.

I remain willing to engage fully in consultation discussions and to review any further information once it becomes available.

## ACAS-aligned position on suitability

### Meaningful Consultation and Ability to Assess Suitability

To assess whether a proposed role is suitable, I need sufficient operational information about how it would work in practice, not only how duties are described in a job description.

At the 18 May consultation meeting, several important practical aspects of the proposed role remained undefined. The ROH follow-up letter of 20 May 2026 records that workload allocation, how operational work would be shared across the team, helpdesk coverage between 8am and 6pm, expectations for part-time staff, and wider team-working arrangements would be discussed once the new manager is in post (expected mid-to-late June). Although I work four days per week, how much office attendance would be required was similarly left for the new manager to discuss.

I was asked to assess suitability and make decisions regarding my employment position, including whether to apply for voluntary redundancy, before these operational terms could be clarified. The voluntary redundancy deadline passed while workload split, helpdesk expectations, office attendance, and how shared work would be allocated remained to be confirmed.

My concern is not resistance to change. I have continued to engage constructively in consultation and to consider the proposal in good faith. The concern is that I have not been able to objectively assess how the role would operate day to day before being asked to treat it as a predetermined direct match and before key employment decisions were required.

The restructure is wide-ranging. The role should not be treated as an automatic direct match without fuller assessment. While there may be some overlap in duties, the proposed role appears to change the scope, structure, expectations, and how my role would work day to day. On that basis, it should be treated as proposed suitable alternative employment requiring a proper suitability assessment.

### Moving operational expectations

- Some tasks overlap on paper, but overlap does not mean the roles are the same in practice.
- At the 18 May consultation meeting, ROH stated they were satisfied the roles were sufficiently similar; I do not agree that has been demonstrated.
- The proposed E&F Support Officer role shifts emphasis toward helpdesk, reactive support, and administrative processing rather than coordinator-level coordination.

### Hybrid working and operational feasibility

- I work part-time (four days), hybrid (two office, two home), with arrangements structured around childcare responsibilities.
- At the start of consultation, flexible working was said to remain unchanged; later, office attendance was said to be for the new manager to decide.
- Expectations on office-based working may have been communicated more clearly to others than to me, despite management knowing my hybrid pattern and childcare commitments.

### Practical concerns and consultation timing

- Helpdesk coverage between 8am and 6pm was raised at consultation, but how that would work for part-time staff remains unclear and was to be discussed with the new manager.
- Workload split, sickness cover, office attendance, and whether staff might be expected outside contracted days were deferred until a new manager is in post (mid-to-late June).
- I could not assess whether the role would work day to day before the voluntary redundancy deadline while these arrangements were still TBC.

### Skills and responsibilities

- The proposed role merges support, helpdesk, and operational functions within an integrated team rather than being my current role renamed.
- Duties such as contractor coordination, budget monitoring, and cross-department coordination appear reduced or reframed as assist/support tasks with escalation to a Manager.
- Familiar wording in places does not restore the same sign-off or practical expectations.

### Overlap in duties already performed

- I recognise overlap in access control coordination, contractor liaison, purchase orders, SharePoint maintenance, and coordination tasks.
- My concern is how duties would work in practice: integrated team working, escalation to Manager, and different sign-off, not mere relabelling of duties I already perform.

### Consultation quality

- I remain concerned that meaningful consultation would allow employees to assess suitability with sufficient operational detail before being asked to treat the role as a predetermined match.
- Key questions on helpdesk hours, office attendance, workload, and team structure could not be answered at the 18 May meeting and remain open.
- I remain concerned that consultation should allow proper assessment of how the role would work in practice before conclusions on suitability are reached.

### Conclusion

The proposed role may contain some familiar duties, but the restructure is wide-ranging. It should not be treated as an automatic direct match without fuller assessment. It appears to be a materially changed or merged role. It should therefore be treated as proposed suitable alternative employment, subject to proper consultation, written clarification, objective comparison, and consideration of my circumstances.

Throughout consultation, I have continued to engage constructively, raise practical questions, and consider the proposal in good faith. My concerns relate to how the proposed role would work in practice, rather than resistance to organisational change itself.
