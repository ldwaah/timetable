### Market tips (facilities + arts crossover)

- **Arts listings are niche** — fewer applicants than LinkedIn “1,000+ Facilities Coordinator London”; check **The Stage**, **Arts Jobs**, **UK Theatre jobs**, venue careers pages weekly.
- **Generic FM is saturated** — CBRE/corporate roles favour corporate FM or IFM backgrounds; still apply, but expect **high applicant counts** (e.g. Arch Insurance listing showed 85+ applicants).
- **Compliance is your edge** — fire, H&S, contractor permits, show-week pressures at ROH map well to **Safety Advisor (NT)**, **UAL compliance**, **ATG Risk Champion** roles.
- **Temp/contract** (Hays, agency) can bridge to June redundancy — useful for cash flow and references.
- **Set alerts** on National Theatre, ATG, Royal Ballet & Opera, Museum of London, City of London (Barbican/Guildhall), NHS estates — hiring is **lumpy** around capital projects and maternity covers.

## Red flags / avoid

| Red flag | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Upfront fees** (DBS, training, “placement”) | Common scam pattern on aggregator sites |
| **Vague “facilities” = cleaning-only** at very low pay | Not career-level FC |
| **Expired ads reposted** on Jobijoba / StudySmarter without apply link to employer | Wasted effort — always apply via **official** careers page |
| **Roles requiring IOSH/NEBOSH essential** if you do not hold them | e.g. Battersea compliance (needed cert); only apply if qualified |
| **Pure engineering / Band 7+ NHS** without team or qualification match | Misaligned level |
| **100% remote “FM coordinator”** with no employer name | Often low-quality or misleading |
| **AI-generated job spam** on unknown domains | Stick to Find a job, Workday, NT jobs, The Stage, jobs.ac.uk |
