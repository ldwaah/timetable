## 3. Human, authentic writing guide (UK)

### UK CV conventions (short)

- **CV** not “resume” in applications (UK HR understands both).  
- **Reverse chronological** experience.  
- **No** first person in bullets (“I managed…”) — implied subject: **Managed**, **Coordinated**, **Liaised**.  
- **Profile:** 4–6 lines, mixed sentence length — one short punchy line is fine.  
- **References:** “Available on request” or omit.  
- **Cover letter:** Often expected for arts/heritage — one page, specific to employer.

### Replace “AI tells” with specifics

| AI tell | Human alternative |
|--------|-------------------|
| spearheaded, leveraged, synergised, championed | led, ran, coordinated, agreed, fixed, chased |
| dynamic, passionate, results-driven | [delete — show results instead] |
| proven track record | [delete — give one example] |
| stakeholder engagement | named groups: “production, development, Front of House” |
| utilised | used |
| responsible for | [verb] + [object] + [scale/outcome] |
| Identical 18-word bullets | Mix: one short bullet; one with a number; one with a tool name |
| No numbers anywhere | At least 3–5 quantified facts on page 1 |

### Voice principles

- Write as you would explain your week to a competent HR person in the arts — **plain, precise, not salesy**.  
- Name **systems** (SharePoint, AutoCAD, Presto) and **regimes** (Heritage England, theatre licensing).  
- One **concrete anecdote** in cover letter beats five adjectives on the CV (e.g. performance night access issue resolved without delaying curtain).  
- **Part-time and hybrid** — state factually if relevant; do not apologise. Frame as: “Experienced delivering coordinator-level workload on a 4-day week.”

### Profile examples — GOOD vs BAD (your profile)

**BAD (generic):**
> Highly motivated Facilities professional with a strong work ethic and excellent communication skills. Adept at multitasking in demanding environments and driving continuous improvement across stakeholder groups.

**GOOD (authentic UK tone):**
> Facilities Coordinator at the Royal Opera House, supporting a listed performance venue where compliance, contractor control, and calm customer service matter as much as the plumbing. I maintain the contractor register, keep facilities documentation and drawings up to date (including AutoCAD), and handle a heavy invoice load with Finance each week. Background in musical theatre before estates; based in Watford, looking for coordinator or estates roles in London and Hertfordshire where show schedules and building safety both count.

## 4. Tailoring playbook (15 minutes per application)

1. **Read the job description** — highlight 8–10 must-have phrases.  
2. **Mirror 5–6 in your profile** (one sentence) and **3–4 in ROH bullets** — only where true.  
3. **Reorder bullets** — top three ROH bullets match their top three duties.  
4. **Rename file** — `Christa-Dawson-CV-[Employer]-[Role].pdf`.  
5. **Cover letter** — 3 short paragraphs:  
   - **Why them** (one specific fact: building, programme, accreditation).  
   - **Why you** (2–3 ROH facts mapped to their JD).  
   - **Practical** (Watford base, 4-day availability or full-time openness, notice period).  

**Commute framing (Watford Junction):**

- Central London: ~20–25 min to Euston — strong for West End, Southbank, City fringe.  
- For Hertfordshire roles: emphasise local base.  
- For deep south/east London: mention willingness to commute or hybrid if offered.

**Roles to de-emphasise unless you want them:**

- Pure helpdesk / service desk (your consultation shows you are **overqualified** and it reads as a step down).  
- If applying anyway, lead with coordinator duties, not “customer service passion.”
