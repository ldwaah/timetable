# Cover letter templates — Facilities professional (redundancy)

*Prepared May 2026. British English. Based on Royal Opera House Facilities Coordinator role documentation in this workspace (`roh-role-analysis/`). No separate CV or `job-search-research.md` / `cv-improvement-guide.md` was found — swap bracketed placeholders where your history differs.*

---

## 1. How to use these letters

**Aim:** one tailored letter in about 10 minutes, not a rewrite from scratch.

| Swap every time | Keep unless wrong for the role |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| `[Employer name]`, `[Role title]`, `[Hiring manager name]` | Your name, contact details, sign-off |
| Opening line (pick from bank or template) | ROH employer name and dates (factual) |
| One paragraph on *why this organisation* (2–3 specific facts from their website/JD) | Core duty examples you actually did |
| One paragraph mapping *their* JD bullets to your evidence | Notice period line (if using Template D) |
| Closing line (from bank) | Watford/commute line (Template E roles only) |

**Do not:** paste your whole CV; repeat every ROH duty; send the same letter to three employers with only the name changed.

**Do:** mirror 3–4 phrases from the job advert; name one building, programme, or compliance point they care about; keep redundancy explanation to one calm sentence unless they ask for more.

---

## 2. Master principles

**UK tone**
- Formal but plain: “I am applying for…” is fine; so is a direct opener (see bank).
- “Organise” not “organize”; “liaise”; “programme” (arts schedule) vs “program” (software only if they use US spelling).
- Avoid American closings (“Sincerely” alone is thin); prefer “Yours sincerely” (named recipient) or “Yours faithfully” (Dear Sir/Madam).

**Length**
- **One page maximum** (roughly 250–350 words for the body).
- Short versions: two paragraphs + sign-off ≈ 120–180 words.

**Structure**
1. Why this role / employer (specific).
2. What you bring (3–4 evidence points — duties, not adjectives).
3. Optional: availability / redundancy (one line).
4. Close: interview + thanks.

**Sign-off**
```
Yours sincerely,

[Your name]
[Phone] | [Email]
```

**Content rules**
- Lead with venue/estates reality: performance schedules, contractor control, compliance, invoices — not “passion for excellence”.
- Musical theatre background: one sentence tying **reliability under show pressure** to FM — not a career digression.
- Redundancy: factual, forward-looking (see Template D). No consultation war stories in applications.

**Words to avoid**
spearheaded, passionate about, thrilled, leverage, synergy, dynamic professional, results-driven, rockstar, hit the ground running (unless quoting their advert ironically — don't).

## 6. Template D — Redundancy / notice period

*Use **one** line in the main letter unless the form asks for more. Stay positive; no HR dispute detail.*

**Standard (recommended)**
> My role at the Royal Opera House is affected by an estates and facilities restructure; I am leaving by agreement at the end of June 2026 and am available to start from [date / reasonable notice].

**Shorter**
> I am leaving the Royal Opera House following redundancy in June 2026 and can start from [date].

**If they ask on the form (slightly more detail)**
> Following a departmental restructure at the Royal Opera House, my Facilities Coordinator post ceases in June 2026. I have engaged fully in consultation, am leaving on agreed terms, and am now focused on my next permanent role.

**If applying before exit is confirmed (careful)**
> I am currently Facilities Coordinator at the Royal Opera House, where my role is under consultation due to restructure; I am exploring suitable external roles with an expected exit around end of June 2026.

**Do not write in cover letters**
- Voluntary redundancy vs suitable alternative employment arguments
- Named managers, slide decks, or “step back” language from consultation
- Flexible working disputes or childcare (save for interview if relevant to hours)

**Interview answer (if asked “what happened?”)**
> “The ROH is restructuring estates and facilities. My coordinator post is being removed and I’ve agreed to leave in June. I’m proud of the work — contractor governance, budgets, compliance in a listed theatre — and I’m looking for the same coordinator-level accountability elsewhere.”

## 11. Checklist before send

- [ ] Employer name, role title, and reference number correct everywhere (including file name if PDF).
- [ ] Hiring manager name spelled right; “sincerely” vs “faithfully” matches salutation.
- [ ] **One page** or less; short version used only if they cap word count.
- [ ] At least **two specifics** from their advert or website (not generic praise).
- [ ] **Three to four duty proofs** from ROH (or [Employer]) — no invented employers or metrics.
- [ ] Redundancy: **one line** unless form asks for more; no consultation detail.
- [ ] MT mention: **one sentence**, tied to ops — only if true.
- [ ] Commute/Watford paragraph only for local roles.
- [ ] Buzzword scan: spearheaded, passionate, thrilled, leverage, synergy, dynamic, results-driven — **removed**.
- [ ] CV alignment: every bold claim in the letter appears on the CV.
- [ ] Contact details and date on the letter; attachment named clearly (`Christa-Surname-Sadlers-Wells-FC.pdf`).
- [ ] Read aloud once — does it sound like you talking to a facilities manager?
